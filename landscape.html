Landscape and Surroundings

Saratoga Springs provides a safe and supportive place for you to follow your own path and nurture your body, mind and soul. It is a place where you can simply be present. This sacred site has been used for centuries by Native Americans to connect with nature and their inner-self. Most everyone who visits Saratoga Springs leaves with a renewed spirit and improved health just from walking our grounds.

The valley ends in three box canyons, each with an abundance of native plant life. The valley floor is home to 45 acres of large oak and walnut trees and meadows. Saratoga Springs boasts plenty of hiking opportunities from short strolls to vigorous 2-3 hour climbs. On trails leading to views of Clearlake, Blue Lakes and the surrounding valleys, you may encounter rabbits, squirrels, quail, deer and many other animals. There are plateaus on the hills and mountains that lend themselves to ceremonies, meditation or stargazing.
